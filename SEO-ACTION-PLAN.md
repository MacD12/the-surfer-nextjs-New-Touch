## 1. Google Search Console (GSC) — set up THIS WEEK

**Why it matters:** GSC is how Google tells you which queries you currently rank for, what your CTR is, and surfaces errors that block indexing.

### Setup steps

1. Go to https://search.google.com/search-console
2. Click "Add property" → "URL prefix" → enter `https://www.thesurferweligama.com`
3. Verify ownership using **DNS TXT record** (preferred — survives platform migrations). Your registrar (where the domain is registered) needs to add a TXT record. Google gives you the exact value to paste.
4. Once verified, submit your sitemap: `https://www.thesurferweligama.com/sitemap.xml`
5. Add both locale versions if Google treats them as separate (you may see `https://www.thesurferweligama.com/en` and `/de` separately under Pages).

### After ~7 days of data, check:

- **Performance tab → Queries.** Filter by country = Germany, UK, Netherlands. See what you currently rank for.
- **Pages tab.** Confirm `/en` and `/de` are indexed. Confirm the new `/en/surf-camp-sri-lanka` landing is indexed within 2 weeks (use "URL inspection" → "Request indexing" to accelerate).
- **Coverage / Pages → Why pages aren't indexed.** Fix any "Excluded" pages that shouldn't be excluded.

### Repeat monthly

- Track ranking position for "Surf Camp Sri Lanka", "Surf Camp Weligama", "Best Surf Camp Sri Lanka". You want these queries on page 1 within 3-6 months.
- Track impressions and clicks for blog posts — they should grow steadily.

## 2. Google Business Profile (GBP) — biggest single LOCAL SEO lever

**Why it matters:** GBP is what puts you in the Google Maps "3-pack" when someone searches "surf camp Sri Lanka" near you, and it's the #1 trust signal Google uses for local businesses. A well-maintained GBP outranks anything else in local search.

### If you don't have one yet

1. Go to https://business.google.com → "Manage now"
2. Search "The Surfer Beach Camp Weligama" — if it appears, claim it. If not, click "Add your business".
3. Use the **exact same** name, address, phone (NAP) that appears in your website footer and structured data. Consistency matters — Google checks.
   - Name: `The Surfer Beach Camp`
   - Address: `NO 65, Wadana Watta, Pelena, Weligama 81700, Sri Lanka`
   - Phone: `+94 76 696 6543`
   - Website: `https://www.thesurferweligama.com/en/beach-camp`
4. Choose category: **Surf school** (primary). Add **Lodging** and **Hostel** as additional categories.
5. Set hours: Mo–Su 07:00–21:00.
6. Verify via postcard (Google mails a code to your physical address). Takes 1–2 weeks.

### Once verified

- **Add 30+ photos**: 10 surf action shots, 10 of accommodation, 5 food, 5 staff/lifestyle. Phones cameras are fine — quality > quantity isn't always true here, freshness matters.
- **Enable booking link** pointing to `/en/rates` or your booking system.
- **Write a 750-character business description** including the exact phrases: "surf camp Sri Lanka", "Weligama", "ISA-certified", "4.9 star rating".
- **Post weekly**: a Google Post is a mini-article that appears on your listing. One per week minimum. Topics: a guest review, a surf condition update, an upcoming event, a new package. This signals "active business".
- **Reply to every review within 48 hours** — both positive and negative. Reply text affects local rank.

### Create separate GBP listings

Each camp gets its own listing — but only if it's at a separate physical address. So:
- The Surfer Beach Camp — its address
- The Surfer TS2 Camp — its address
- Soul Surfer Camp — its address

Three listings = three chances to appear in local pack. Same NAP discipline applies to each.

## 3. Reviews — the highest-leverage off-page signal you can move

You have 1,600+ Google reviews at 4.9★ — incredible. Keep the momentum.

### Action

- **Email every check-out guest** within 48 hours of their departure with a one-click Google review link. Auto-send.
- Generate your Google review link: open your GBP listing → Share → "Get review form". Use that URL in the email.
- Translate the email request into German for German guests — increases response rate noticeably.
- Don't ask for 5-star reviews explicitly. Ask for "honest feedback". Google penalises review-gating.

### Target

- Aim for 3-5 fresh reviews per week. Recency matters — Google weights recent reviews higher than 5-year-old ones.

### Also collect reviews on

- **Tripadvisor** — you already do, keep going.
- **Booking.com** if you list there — reviews here help your Booking.com ranking (more direct bookings via that channel).
- **Trustpilot** — useful for European trust signals.

## 4. Backlinks — the slow but compounding ranking signal

**Why it matters:** Google still uses links as a major ranking signal. Quality > quantity. A link from one travel publication beats 100 links from random directories.

### Easy wins (you can do these today)

| Site | Action | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| **Surfline directory** | Submit your camp at https://www.surfline.com/surf-spots/sri-lanka/weligama-bay-12468 community section | High DA, surf-relevant |
| **Magicseaweed (now Surfline)** | Same — submit camp + write a spot description for Weligama | Topical authority |
| **Surftrip directory** | Submit at surftrip.com | Niche-relevant |
| **The Inertia** | Pitch them an article — "How Sri Lanka became Europe's winter surf destination" | High-authority surf media |
| **Lonely Planet** | Add yourself to their Weligama lodging listings | High-authority travel |
| **Wikipedia** — Weligama article | Cite The Surfer Beach Camp as one of the named surf operators in the Weligama Bay surf section (with a reliable source citation) | Hard to get but huge once landed |
| **Sri Lanka Tourism Board** | Apply to be listed | Local trust signal |

### Mid-effort (PR / outreach)

- Pitch yourself to surf bloggers who cover Sri Lanka. Offer them a free week's stay in exchange for an honest write-up with a backlink. Targets: SurferToday, Wavelength, German "Surf Magazin", "Prime Surfing" (DE).
- Pitch to travel bloggers writing about Europe-to-Asia winter trips. Same offer.

### Avoid

- Paid link farms / "buy 1000 backlinks for €50". Google's algorithm will detect and penalise.
- Comment spam on blogs/forums.
- Excessive directory submissions to low-quality sites.

## 8. What NOT to do

- ❌ Don't keyword-stuff existing copy on your camp pages. Google penalises unnatural repetition. The keyword density we set is already tuned.
- ❌ Don't buy reviews or links. Both get penalised, sometimes severely.
- ❌ Don't change page URLs ever — every change kills accumulated SEO equity.
- ❌ Don't duplicate content across pages (e.g. paste the same FAQ on all 3 camp pages). The per-camp FAQs we shipped are DELIBERATELY DIFFERENT for each camp.
- ❌ Don't ignore the GBP. It's the single biggest local SEO lever.
- ❌ Don't run aggressive "thin" landing pages (one paragraph + a CTA). Google's helpful-content updates demote these.
